# Product Discovery Research

Use this skill when an idea needs evidence before product or engineering commitment. The output should make the decision clearer, not produce generic brainstorming.

## Workflow

1. Define the target segment, use case, and decision the user is trying to make.
2. Find prior art and current alternatives when market/tool facts may have changed.
3. Map alternatives: incumbent tools, DIY workflows, non-consumption, adjacent products, and internal workarounds.
4. Separate facts from inference. Put links near factual claims when browsing is used.
5. Identify the riskiest assumptions: demand, workflow fit, willingness to pay or switch, technical feasibility, trust/safety, and distribution.
6. Look for contradiction: strong tools that failed, ignored features, low-usage product surfaces, or user behavior that undermines the idea.
7. Recommend the next learning step: interview, artifact test, fake door, concierge workflow, prototype, or kill/park.

## Output

```text
Decision To Make:
User / Job:
Current Alternatives:
Evidence Table:
Non-Obvious Insight:
Riskiest Assumptions:
Verdict:
Next Research Or Test:
```

## Rules

- Do not overclaim from marketing pages.
- Prefer observed behavior over stated preference.
- If evidence is weak, say what is unknown instead of filling gaps with confidence.
- For AI-agent products, check whether the bottleneck is model quality, workflow trust, integrations, or user habit.
- For Life OS product work, compare against the cost of keeping the workflow manual inside Plane/Markdown.
