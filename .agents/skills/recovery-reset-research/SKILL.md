# Recovery Reset Research

## Purpose

Build a decision-grade reset plan, not generic rest advice. Treat "I need a retreat" as a hypothesis to compare against sleep repair, workload redesign, vacation, sabbatical, nature/physical challenge, digital restriction, therapy/coaching, and career/job redesign.

## Workflow

1. Reconstruct the user's current state: red flags, sleep/appetite/weight, work aversion, runway, obligations, and what kind of growth feels missing.
2. Separate mechanisms:
   - physiological depletion: sleep, stress load, illness, nutrition, exercise;
   - occupational mismatch: workload, control, reward, community, fairness, values;
   - motivation/meaning: autonomy, competence, relatedness, agency, intellectual load;
   - identity update: peer comparison, failed project, weak signal, "my work did not compound";
   - environment reset: physical context, social context, tools, constraints.
3. Review evidence by tier:
   - Tier A: systematic reviews, meta-analyses, RCTs, official/clinical sources;
   - Tier B: primary theory papers and validated models;
   - Tier C: practitioner books with plausible mechanisms;
   - Tier D: biographies/anecdotes as idea generators only.
4. For each intervention, extract:
   - what it plausibly fixes;
   - effect horizon: same day, 1-2 weeks, 1-3 months, 6+ months;
   - evidence strength;
   - risks/contraindications;
   - compatibility with the user's runway, marriage, Russia/relocation constraints, and work situation.
5. Compare reset options in a matrix:
   sleep repair, normal vacation, offline vacation, nature trip/hike, meditation retreat, physical challenge, sabbatical/light work month, job redesign, job exit search, research/product proof-point sprint.
6. Produce:
   - source map;
   - decision model;
   - 2-3 candidate protocols;
   - falsification metrics for 14/30/60 days;
   - concrete next action.

## Guardrails

- Do not diagnose. State when symptoms warrant clinician/therapist/doctor input.
- Do not overclaim digital detox, retreats, or hiking. Treat them as mechanisms to test.
- Do not moralize envy or work aversion.
- Do not recommend quitting unless runway, obligations, and exit options support it.
- Keep the user's desired life shape in view: money, intellectual work, agency, meaningful work, international mobility, and work that can grow into something important.
