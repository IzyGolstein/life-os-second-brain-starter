# Motivation Letter Admissions

## Core Principle

Write the letter as a coherent admissions argument, not a biography and not a CV in prose. The committee should understand: why this field, why this program, why the user is prepared, what he wants to study, and what he expects to gain.

## Workflow

1. Read the target program criteria first. Extract mandatory content, word limit, scoring weights, and named directions/tracks.
2. Read the user's rough story and the current CV/evidence. Preserve his voice and factual claims; do not invent publications, awards, grades, English level, or social activities.
3. Split material into:
   - `Use in letter`: motivation, turning points, research interests, program fit, future goals.
   - `Use only as evidence`: CV facts, certificates, transcript details.
   - `Ask/flag`: facts that need exact names, confidentiality checks, or proof.
4. Build a 5-part structure:
   - opening intellectual motivation;
   - preparation through education and work;
   - research turn and thesis;
   - current research interests and program fit;
   - expectations from the program and future goals.
5. Keep work experience admissions-relevant: incentives, budget constraints, demand/elasticity, forecasting, market analysis, experiments, causal inference, econometrics, policy.
6. For Russian letters, use a mature academic tone: concrete, reflective, not inflated. Avoid startup-style metrics, self-praise, and generic admiration.
7. Check against the criteria before finalizing: every required item should be present exactly once, without padding.

## Style Rules

- Prefer "я понял", "я увидел", "этот опыт изменил" only when tied to a concrete episode.
- Avoid weak abstractions: "уникальная возможность", "высокий уровень программы", "всегда мечтал".
- Avoid overclaiming: if a fact is not proven, write method/experience rather than achievement.
- Keep the main story narrow enough to be memorable: economics/econometrics plus labor, health, well-being, incentives, and human behavior.
- Do not over-explain CV entries; the letter should interpret the trajectory.
