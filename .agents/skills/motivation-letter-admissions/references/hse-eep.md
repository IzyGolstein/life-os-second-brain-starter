# HSE "Экономика и экономическая политика" Motivation Letter

Source in project: `projects/masters-applications-2026/requirements/economics_and_economic_policy_portfolio_criteria_2026.pdf`.

## Hard Requirements

- Up to 1000 words.
- Must disclose:
  - scientific interests and planned research area;
  - interest in this specific program;
  - expectations from studying on the program;
  - ranking of directions/tracks, with preferred direction justified;
  - further development and goals after graduation;
  - theoretical and practical skills the applicant expects to acquire.

## Program Structure To Mention

Official program pages describe four tracks:

1. `Прикладная экономика` (общая траектория).
2. `Экономические исследования` (исследовательская траектория).
3. `Количественные методы в экономике и финансах` (общая траектория).
4. `Экономическое и финансовое поведение` (исследовательская траектория).

The public program page also says the core includes Microeconomics, Macroeconomics, Econometrics, and Python, and that all tracks emphasize fundamental and applied research, economic analysis, data work, and modeling economic processes.

## Scoring Implication

Higher scores go to letters that show understanding of the topic and key features of the preferred direction, plus high motivation for this specific program. The letter should not merely say "I am interested"; it should demonstrate fit through research questions, methods, and preparation.

## Recommended the user Angle

- Start with curiosity about society at individual and macro levels.
- Use economics as the discipline that turns complex social systems into models useful beyond finance: labor markets, health policy, organ-exchange/market design, incentives, education, well-being.
- Explain early work as a constraint-driven path, not a detour: courier incentives, market forecasting, cashback personalization all taught incentives, budgets, demand, elasticity, and behavioral response.
- Show the academic turn through supervisor interaction, thesis work, data search, and Lomonosov presentation.
- Current interests: applied econometrics, labor economics, health economics, well-being, behavioral economics/biology, causal inference, experiments, panel data.
- Program fit: economics/economic policy, quantitative empirical methods, applied micro/labor/health/well-being, policy relevance.
- Avoid making bioinformatics the main story; use it as breadth that supports interest in human behavior and health.
